boogiepop phantom (gore)

i started watching Boogiepop Phantom because of Paranoia Agent, which was the first anime i had ever seen, in middle school (?). Paranoia Agent was syndicated on [adult swim], which is how my cousin chris first saw it, and then he showed it to me. this was a mistake, because it lead me down a path from which i have never recovered. (i love using the word "which".)

anyways, fast forward a few years, and i really wanted to watch something similar to Paranoia Agent. i read on some forum that fans had considered there to be an unofficial trilogy between Paranoia Agent, Boogiepop Phantom, and Serial Experiments Lain. i have also seen Lain, though idk if before or after Boogiepop. i also did not understand Lain at all. now that i think about it tho, i don't think that i really understood Boogiepop very well either, at least at the time of watching it. however, the soundscaping, general visual aesthetic, and action puilled me in and held my attention.

this anime follows a mystery. like Serial Experiments Lain, the story revolves around highschool students who disappear, and supernatural forces with unclear intentions. like Paranoia Agent, there are vigilante heroes and questionable realities.

tho i do not remember much of the plot, the visual aesthetic and psychological tension of this show heavily shaped my current point of view in my music.

12 ep (easy watching!). dark, brooding aesthetic (uses a lot of those vignetted filters that you see in cop show flashbacks; i find this slightly frustrating, but it's worth it to stick with it bc this is a GOOD show). 90's animation style; looks a little dated now, but again, it's worth it. BEAUTIFUL sound environments; similar to a light-handed take on AHS soundscaping, mostly rock/metal music, but i believe there's at least one sick elctro song in there. mystery/psychological triller/horror. i've started re-watching it a million times, but i think i've only seen it one (maybe two) times. there are also manga. i also just found out that there's a live-action movie (Boogiepop and Others), so i may watch that soon.
